01 / Abstract
Overview

Solid tumors are ecosystems. Where malignant epithelium meets infiltrating immune cells — the invasive front — is where response and resistance to therapy are decided.

We profile biliary tumors with 10x Visium, then run a spot-level pipeline: quality control, marker-based classification into tumor / immune / stromal / transitional niches, spatial-domain clustering, and detection of the tumor–immune interface. Replicate sections are stacked into a pseudo-3D model, and matched pre- and post-treatment biopsies are placed side by side.

Spot

Invasive front

TLS
Tertiary lymphoid structures — organized immune aggregates scored by a 12-chemokine signature.

03 / Pipeline
From counts to a navigable tumor
01
QC & normalization
Filter shallow / high-mito spots, drop rare genes, then library-size normalize (CP10k) and log-transform.
02
Spot classification
Score lineage marker panels per spot and assign a niche: tumor, immune, stromal, or transitional.
03
Spatial domains
Leiden clustering on a kNN graph groups spots into coherent tissue domains labelled by dominant class.
04
Invasive front
On the spatial neighbor graph, flag tumor spots touching immune spots (and vice-versa) as the interface.
05
Interface signaling & TLS
Score checkpoint (PD1/PD-L1), CXCL12–CXCR4 and TGF-β at the front; map tertiary lymphoid structures.
06
3D stacking & pre/post
Stack replicate sections into a pseudo-3D model and compare matched baseline vs on-treatment biopsies.
04 / Reading the atlas
What each color mode shows
Cell-type class
Every spot colored by its niche — tumor, immune, stromal, transitional. The overall tissue architecture.
Invasive front
Highlights tumor core, immune zones, and the green interface band where they collide.
Checkpoint (PD1/PD-L1)
Immune-checkpoint signaling — where the tumor may be suppressing an anti-tumor response.
CXCL12–CXCR4
A chemokine axis linked to immune exclusion and stromal recruitment at the tumor edge.
TGF-β
Immunosuppressive stromal program that can blunt therapy — concentrated in fibrotic regions.
TLS signature
Lights up organized immune aggregates — a candidate marker of favorable response.
